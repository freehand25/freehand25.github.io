Project

Key Advantages

When transferring embodied agents from an original environment (source domain) to unseen environments (target domains) with visual gaps such as changes in lighting, object appearance, background, or unseen distractors, previous methods rely on labor-intensive human demonstrations typically collected via tele-operation. This process is both time-consuming and costly, limiting autonomy and scalability. Our framework, FreeHand, enables adaptation to new environments using only automatically collected RGB images without requiring any human demonstrations. These images can be captured within minutes, making the process labor-free and easy to deploy. With zero human demonstrations, FreeHand achieves a 34% improvement in success rate after less than 9 minutes of data collection, surpassing the 26% gain from finetuning with 130 human demonstrations collected in about 120 minutes. Our method advances automation and self-reliance in embodied learning.

Method

An overview of FreeHand: an adversarial dual-alignment approach which explicitly aligns both visual representations and policy distributions. In this min-max framework, the domain discriminators (maximizer) aim to distinguish between source and target domains, while the target model (minimizer) learns to produce domain-invariant representations.
